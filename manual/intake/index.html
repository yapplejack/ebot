Intake Overview



Mechanism 3 Architecture:


The intake uses 4 pieces of hex shaft a good amount of purchased parts:


  1. Cut plastic back panel and assemble the Ultra Planetary gearbox.
  2. Cut 04_Intake_Roller_Shafts and tap them to 10-32. Cut and tap the 04_Intake_Cluster_Shaft or instead use the leftover hex outshaft included with the REV Max Planetary gearbox.
  3. Manufacture PVC spacers.
  4. Push bearings into 04_Intake_Plates.
  5. Add standoffs and combine 04_Intake_Plates with bolts.
  6. Bolt 04_Intake_Plates into pre drilled holes on 03_Arm_Main_Links, drilling out the remaining holes as necessary.
  7. Remove bolts from standoffs on the outer 04_Intake_Plates
  8. Add ten 2” squishy wheels to each  04_Intake_Roller_Shaft
  9. Put  04_Intake_Roller_Shafts through one the inside 04_Intake_Plates and attach relevant hardware, then add the outer 04_Intake_Plate.
  10. Put  04_Intake_Roller_Shafts through one the other inside 04_Intake_Plates and attach relevant hardware, then add the final outer 04_Intake_Plate.
  11. Add plastic panel between inside 04_Intake_Plates and retighten all spacer bolts.
  12. Attach to 03_Arm_Main_Links.



Intake Machining


The plastic panel needs to be cut to 18” x 5.5”. One set of these holes could be drilled ahead of time but we will hold off until later.


The intake has two sides, each with a different combination of pulleys and gears on the different intake rollers and shafts. For best functionality and reliability it is ideal if these components are lined up nicely and cannot disengage with each other. To help keep everything lined up, a few PVC spacers are used:


  • Qty(2) 0.8” sections to cover some sections of bare hex shaft
  • Qty(2) spacers used to keep the 36 tooth gears in line. The thickness of these spacers depends on the exact gears you are using. The spacers should be 0.8” - [the thickness of your 36T gears].
  • Qty(3) spacers used to keep the 24 tooth pulleys in line. The thickness of these spacers depends on the exact pulleys you are using. The spacers should be 0.8” - [the thickness of your 24T pulleys].


Finally cut all hex shafts used for the intake to length using the cutlist and tap them to 10-32. If you wish to avoid making the 04_Intake_Cluster_Shaft you can instead use the leftover hex outshaft included with the REV Maxplanetary gearbox with a shaft collar to retain the pulley.


UltraPlanetary assembly

Tools required:

  • 3/32 allen wrench
  • 5/64 allen wrench


Parts required:

  • Qty(1) x NEO 550 motor
  • Qty(1) x UltraPlanetary 550 Motor Pinion Gear (comes in pack of two)
  • Qty(1) x UltraPlanetary 5mm Hex Output
  • Qty(1) x UltraPlanetary 550 Motor Plate
  • Qty(1) x UltraPlanetary 5:1 Cartridge
  • Qty(1) x UltraPlanetary 1/2” Hex Adaptor
  • Qty(1) x UltraPlanetary Hardware Pack




Begin assembling the UltraPlanetary gearbox by pressing the UltraPlanetary 550 Motor Pinion onto the NEO 550’s output shaft using an arbor press or similar. A video guide showing how to do this can be found here. REV recommends securing the pinion with Loctite 635 or 638 (“Green” Loctite) or equivalent.

Take the UltraPlanetary 550 Motor Plate and place the flat side against the face of the NEO 550. Line up the two larger mounting holes with the NEO 550 mounting holes and screw it in place with two M3 8mm button head screws.


Line up the input of the UltraPlanetary 5:1 Cartridge with the motor’s pinion and push the cartridge into place, twisting it until the splines and alignment features line up and sit flush.



Use five M3x10mm to secure the ½” hex output to the output stage of the Ultraplanetary.


Take the UltraPlanetary 5mm Hex Output and line it up with the output of the 5:1 cartridge. The output of the Output can be held in place or rotated to make this easier.


Bolt the Hex Output cartridge, 5:1 gear cartridge, and Motor Plate together using six M3 20mm socket head screws. Make sure not to overtighten these screws, they should be finger tight.

Intake Assembly


 Lay out the four 04_Intake_Plates as shown. All four plates are identical




Insert 15 1/2” Hex Flanged Bearings into the plates in the locations shown. Some holes only have bearings in specific plates due to how the intake is assembled.

Add five 1” long 10-32 standoffs to each of what will become the outside intake plates using ten 10-32 3/8” bolts. Four of the locations match between both sides while the fifth is different between sides.



Bolt the two sides together using 10 more 10-32 3/8” bolts in the standoffs and then attach it to the preexisting hole(s) in your 03_Arm_Driver_Links using 10-32 1-1/2” bolts and 10-32 nuts. If you only have a single thru hole in your 03_Arm_Driver_Links you will need to clamp the 04_Intake_Plates into the correct position to drill out the remaining holes in the box tube. There are small slits in at the end of the 04_Intake_Plates to insert shims into to help align the intake properly. If you do not have a small piece of metal to fit through them, use them as guides like shown below to correctly locate the 04_Intake_Plates. Once in the correct position drill out the remaining holes in the intake on each side of each 03_Arm_Driver_Link individually (i.e. not making thru holes). Once the holes have been drilled out, remove the bolts attaching the intake and remove the 10-32 3/8” bolts in the standoffs on the outside 04_Intake_Plates.


Take the three 04_Intake_Roller_Shafts and space out ten 2” Thrifty Squish Wheels on each. If you find the wheels are being displaced by the cone, add additional 0.75” wide PVC spacers to maintain the spacing of the wheels.


Bolt the Ultra Planetary gearbox to the inside plate on the right side of the intake using six M3 10mm bolts, which should be left over from the Ultra Planetary kit. The gearbox will sit fully inside the intake side plate assembly, with just the shaft extending in between the two right side plates.



Add a 24 Tooth HTD Pulley to the Ultra Planetary output shaft. Insert the first 04_Intake_Roller_Shaft with wheels added into the same 04_Intake_Plate in the bearing closest to the Ultra Planetary as shown, lining up the hex shaft with the hex bearing. On this 04_Intake_Roller_Shaft add one of the PVC pulley spacers and a 24 Tooth HTD Pulley. Connect the two 24 tooth pulleys with a 60 Tooth HTD Timing Belt.



Insert the second 04_Intake_Roller_Shaft into the next closest bearing along the bottom of the intake and add a 36 Tooth gear and one of the PVC gear spacers. Put 04_Intake_Cluster_Shaft through the additional higher bearing on this side of the intake. On the inside of the intake, add a 24 Tooth HTD pulley so that it is in line with the pulley on the frontmost 04_Intake_Roller_Shaft and cap the shaft off with a 10-32 3/8” bolt and #10 washer. On the outside of the intake, add a 36 Tooth Gear and PVC gear spacer in line with the gear and spacer on the second 04_Intake_Roller_Shaft. Finally, add a 24 Tooth HTD pulley to the end of the last 04_Intake_Roller_Shaft and drape the 60 Tooth HTD Timing Belt around it before pushing it through the frontmost bearing in the 04_Intake_Plate. Add one of the 0.8” PVC spacers on the outside. Run the 60T belt from the last 04_Intake_Roller_Shaft pulley to the 04_Intake_Cluster_Shaft pulley. If the REV Ultra Planetary gearbox output shaft is used the pulleys on the inside of the intake can be retained with a shaft collar instead. The output shaft of the Ultra Planetary may need to be cut down to move past the supports.


Once all the hardware is in place, add the outer 04_Intake_Plate by lining up all the hex bearings with the hex shafts sticking out from the inner plate. Add 10-32 3/8” bolts onto 1” the standoffs and cap all the tapped hex shafts with #10 washers and 10-32 3/8” bolts.























On the left side of the intake, run the three 04_Intake_Roller_Shafts through the inner 04_Intake_Plate. A 150 Tooth HTD Timing Belt will run from the innermost 04_Intake_Roller_Shaft to the outermost 04_Intake_Roller_Shaft. Add 24 Tooth HTD Pulleys to both shafts with PVC pulley spacers capping them off. The middle 04_Intake_Roller_Shaft will require a 0.8” PVC spacer.








Once all the hardware is in place add the outer 04_Intake_Plate and add 10-32 3/8” bolts onto the standoffs. Cap all the tapped hex shaft with #10 washers and 10-32 3/8” bolts.








Remove the two uppermost standoffs on both sides of the intake. Mark a line 1” in from the edge on both sides of the plastic panel and then clamp the panel inside of the intake such that it lines up with the two removed standoff holes. Once clamped on both sides, drill through the holes in the 04_Intake_Plates and into the plastic panel with a #7 drill bit and re-add the standoffs and 10-32 3/8” bolts with the plastic panel now in the stackup.


Once the plastic panel is attached the intake can be reattached to the 03_Arm_Driver_Links using 10-32 1-1/2” bolts and 10-32 locknuts. The intake can alternatively be riveted to the robot which will cut down on weight but drilling out the rivets may make reattaching it more difficult if changes need to be made or something needs to be replaced later. If the 04_Intake_Roller_Shafts are oversized shaft collars can be used to help retain the position of everything.
