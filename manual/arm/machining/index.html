Arm Machining







Make two of these 03_Arm_Driver_Links. The two smaller holes will be added when the Hex Hubs and Thrifty Bearing Blocks are installed later. Make sure to offset the hole by 1/16” off the centerline (15/16” from the edge of the tube) instead of centering it across the 2” face.















Make two of these 03_Arm_Main_Links.


On the right hand side of the drawing, showing the top face of 03_Arm_Main_Link,, only drill out the hole in the center of the cluster that is marked with dimensions. The other holes will be added later when gusseting. If you do not have access to a drill press it may be best not to make this a thru hole and instead only drill through the top face, waiting until the top gusset is installed to line up the gusset on the bottom of 03_Arm_Main_Link. If using a drill press, clamp both pieces on top of each other and drill down through both parts, making sure to line them up on the bottom edge closest to the hole.




For the holes on the left side of the drawing, showing the side of 03_Arm_Main_Link where the intake will eventually attach, you will want to grab an AndyMark T gusset or any other gusset that has dimensions that match the spacing of the holes. Any gusset or bracket with holes close to 0.2” in diameter and 1” apart should work. We will use the gusset as a template to drill out these intake attachment holes. Mark out the first hole, which is centered across the 1” face and 0.5” away from the edge, and then line up your gusset on top.



If a drill press is being used, clamp the two pieces together, making sure to line up the ends closest to the first hole drilled. After clamping the tube stock together, clamp your gusset on and drill using a #10 through both tubes using the four rightmost holes in the gusset pictured below.



If there is no access to a drill press, clamp the gusset to a single 03_Arm_Main_Link and make THRU hole on the 4th hole rightmost hole in the picture above. Do this for the other 03_Arm_Main_Link.
