Arm Assembly

Tools required:
  • 5/32” allen wrench
  • 1/8” allen wrench
  • Electric drill
  • #7 (or close equivalent 13/64) drill bit
  • #10 drill bit
  • Rivet gun


Approximate Parts required:
  • Qty(2) x 03_Arm_Main_Link
  • Qty(1) x 03_Arm_Cross
  • Qty(2) x 03_Arm_Driver_Link
  • Qty(1) x 03_Arm_Closeout
  • Qty(2) x 03_Arm_Pivot_Shaft
  • Qty(1) x 03_Arm_Tension_Unit
  • Qty(2) x 90 Degree Gussets
  • Qty(6) x T Gussets
  • Qty(2) x McMaster-Carr 1528A21 Door Hinge
  • Qty(2) x Piano Hinge
  • Qty(16) x 8-32 3/8” button head bolts
  • Qty(4) x 10-32 3/8” bolts
  • #10 washers
  • 3/16 rivets


Start by placing a Thrifty Clamping Bearings Block around the large hole towards the end of both 03_Arm_Driver_Link. The slight offset of the large hole in the Clamping Bearing Blocks should line up with the slight offset of the 1.125” hole. Loosely attach them with the included bolts and then take two 1/2” Hex Hubs and insert them into either side of the bearing blocks instead of bearings. The hub should fit within the hole made in the 03_Arm_Driver_Link. Bolt the 1/2” Hex Hubs to the Clamping Bearing Blocks using four 8-32 3/8” button head screws and then tighten the bolts in the Thrifty Clamping Bearing Blocks.


Hold the arm assembly up and into the superstructure frame and line up the Thrifty Clamping Bearing Block assemblies on the 03_Arm_Driver_Link and 02_Superstructure_Main_Beam. If you have not already cut your 03_Arm_Pivot_Shafts, to get the best fit, slip what remains of Stick 1 of the 1/2” hex shaft through both sets of bearing blocks on one side of the arm, adding a 1/16” long spacer with 1/2” hex bore in between the blocks. If the hex shaft is not easily going through the bearing block stackup, the bolts on the inner hex hub and outside of the clamping bearing blocks can easily be loosened and then retightened after the shaft is slid into place. Then add a 24 tooth sprocket onto the end of the hex shaft outside of the Superstructure frame. Make sure to press everything on the shaft together. Push the shaft in deep enough so one end is flush with either the outer hex hub or the sprocket, depending on which is easier, and then mark the other end of the shaft so it will end up flush against both the hex hub and sprocket when cut. Cut the hex shaft and repeat on the other side with the rest of Stick 1 to get perfectly sized 03_Arm_Pivot_Shafts. Tap the  03_Arm_Pivot_Shafts for a 10-32 bolt.


If you have already cut your 03_Arm_Pivot_Shafts, you may find them to be slightly short or long depending on the construction of your robot so far. If they are slightly short, insert the shafts so that they favor sitting flush against the 24 tooth sprocket, fully engaging with the hex bore and sitting slightly recessed inside the 1/2” Hex Hub.






(Re)insert the 03_Arm_Pivot_Shafts through the bearing blocks with bearings on the Superstructure, add the 1/16” length spacer, and finally line the shaft up with the bore of the hex hubs on the 03_Arm_Driver_Link. Add a 24 tooth sprocket onto 03_Arm_Pivot_Shaft on the outside face of 02_Superstructure_Main_Beam and cap off both ends of both hex shafts with a 10-32 3/8” bolt and #10 washer on each side to hold the 03_Arm_Pivot_Shaft in place.


Check to make sure that the arm swings freely and is fairly centered between the superstructure. None of the bolt heads in the Clamping Bearing Block assemblies should rub or hit each other.


With the two 03_Arm_Driver_Link affixed to the robot, we will need to install 03_Arm_Closeout between them. Line up two Andymark Tee gussets with the unattached end of 03_Arm_Driver_Link, then drill with a #7 and rivet the three holes closest to the bearing blocks, leaving the two holes closest to the end of the 03_Arm_Driver_Links unriveted.









Then, on the underside of the arm, drill out and rivet in two 90 degree gussets with one section of the gusset on 03_Arm_Closeout and the other pointing towards the bearing blocks on 03_Arm_Driver_Link. Make sure to file the corners of the 90 degree gusset if they protrude over the edge of the box tube, or they may tear the tensioning cable that will be attached in the finishing touches section.






Once the Tee gussets are in place, you will need to add the U-bolts into the unriveted holes at the end of 03_Arm_Driver_Link. Depending on the exact U-bolts purchased, the holes may need to be drilled wider and/or the legs of the U-bolt can be pushed closer in a vice. When attaching the U-bolts, first tighten the provided nuts all the way down, insert the provided plate, put the U bolt through the gusset and box tube, and then install extra 1/4-20 (or whatever is appropriate for your U bolt) nuts. The nuts on the inside may take a bit of patience to get in.




Take the two 03_Arm_Main_Links and lay them out with 03_Arm_Cross in between. Clamp two T Gussets onto the top so that the center hole of the Tee gusset lines up with the single predrilled hole in 03_Arm_Main_Links. Place a rivet in the center hole without riveting it to hold the gusset in the right position and then clamp the T gusset in place. Drill out the remaining holes and complete by riveting the 3/16” rivets.










After attaching the top T gussets grab a piece of box tube or other straight edge and butt the end closest to the T gusset of 03_Arm_Main_Links against it to keep them square. Add 03_Arm_Cross in between the T gussets, making sure that the assembly is as squared as possible. Due to the edge of the Andymark T gussets it may be difficult to use a speed square to check if all of the corners are square, but a setup like the one pictured should assist.

Once the top rivet holes are drilled out with a #7 drill bit and the 3/16” rivets are in place, use two more T gussets on the other side. These should be easier to install as the assembly is already held together.




Now bolt a strap hinge to the 03_Arm_Driver_Links below the 90 degree gussets connected to 03_Arm_Closeout. Make sure that the pivot point of the strap hinge lines up the end of the box tube. Once in place use a #7 drill bit and 3/16” rivets to secure in place. Please keep an eye on these rivets as they may wear over time, 10-32 bolts could be used here instead.


Position the assemblies so that the 03_Arm_Main_Links and 03_Arm_Driver_Link are lined up with one another. The two crossbars, 03_Arm_Cross and 03_Arm_Closeout, should both be closer to where the two assemblies meet. When attaching the 03_Arm_Main_Links, try to leave no gap or as small a gap as possible between them and the 03_Arm_Driver_Links. Use a #7 and 3/16” rivets once again.




If the assemblies drastically do not line up due to the length of 03_Arm_Cross it is totally fine to cut it down slightly to fit better, just know this affects the length of hex shaft used later on the intake.  Make sure to resquare the 03_Arm_Cross when reattaching.




























Cut a strip 23” long by 10” wide out of the 1/8” thick polycarb sheet to form the 03_Arm_Tension_Unit that will eventually be mounted to 03_Arm_Cross. 03_Arm_Tension_Unit has a top side and a bottom side as well as an upper edge and a lower edge. The top side is the side of the Lexan sheet that will be facing up when the arm is extended, with the bottom side facing down. The upper edge is the edge of the Lexan that will be hinged off of the 02_Superstructure_Top_Crossbeam on the static superstructure. The lower edge is the edge of the Lexan that will be hinged off of the 03_Arm_Cross on the swinging arm that the intake is attached to.

Carefully cut both Piano Hinges down to 10” long on a bandsaw. The Piano Hinge on the upper end is riveted to the bottom of 03_Arm_Tension_Unit with the axle of the hinge protruding in the direction of the heads of the rivets. The Piano Hinge on the lower end is riveted to the top of 03_Arm_Tension_Unit with the axle of the hinge protruding away from the heads of the rivets. We used wide-headed rivets on our version, the included rivets should work fine but a #10 washer can be used alongside the rivet for additional rigidity.



If you are struggling with the images above it might be helpful to clamp the polycarbonate to the piano hinges and then clamp the assembly to the robot. On the upper edge you should see 03_Arm_Tension_Unit with the piano hinge behind it, with the axle of the hinge pointing away and the other flap of the hinge against the inwards facing side of 02_Superstructure_Top_Crossbeam. On the lower edge you should see the piano hinge in front of both the 03_Arm_Tension_Unit and 03_Arm_Cross with the axle of the hinge pointing away from you.


Before mounting to the robot four additional holes made with a #7 drill bit are put through 03_Arm_Tension_Unit and the Piano Hinges. These holes will be used to correctly tension the Everybot’s arm and are in line with the Piano Hinge’s premade holes and between the two outer holes, slightly closer to the outermost hole. The picture below shows the location for these holes on a Piano Hinge.



To finally mount 03_Arm_Tension_Unit, line up the middle of the other flap of the hinge at the bottom edge of 03_Arm_Tension_Unit with the middle of 03_Arm_Cross. Position the hinge on the front face of 03_Arm_Cross so the axle of the hinge hangs over the edge and 03_Arm_Tension_Unit can rotate freely. The top of 03_Arm_Tension_Unit should face the holes for attaching the intake in 03_Arm_Main_Link and the bottom should face the hinges connecting 03_Arm_Main_Link and 03_Arm_Driver_Link. Clamp the hinge in place and rivet it using 3/16” rivets.




The hinge riveted to the upper edge of the bottom surface of 03_Arm_Tension_Unit should be bolted to 02_Superstructure_Top_Crossbeam. Lift the arm so that the upper edge of 03_Arm_Tension_Unit is brought up to the bottom of 02_Superstructure_Top_Crossbeam. Measure out and line up the middles of the 02_Superstructure_Top_Crossbeam and Piano Hinge and mark and drill out #10 thru holes in 02_Superstructure_Top_Crossbeam. Bolt the hinge into place using five 10-32 1-1/2” bolts and 10-32 locknuts so it is easy to remove if the arm needs to be taken off later.


Please note the pictures above are using rope, this has been replaced by steel cable in the final build. See the Finishing Touches section for more details.



To finish off the arm section we will need to add the chain that runs from the sprockets on the 02_Superstructure_Jackshaft to the sprockets on the 03_Arm_Pivot_Shafts. What you might normally think about as a link of chain actually comes in two variants - an “inside” link and an “outside” link. If you look closely at roller chain, you will see that the “sides” of each alternating link are either inside or outside of the two next to it.


Looking at the #25 roller chain, find the first section of chain that has pins through both holes of an outer section (in the photo above, this would be the second link in from the left). Set up the chain in the #25 chain tool so that the pin closer to the free end of the chain in this outer section is lined up with the channel cut towards the bottom of the tool (usually towards the left of the tool). The shorter bolt that extends into the channel cut upwards into the tool (usually to the right) is tightened down to the chain to hold it in place (and used in reassembling the chain later) and the longer bolt with a pin attached is tightened down onto the chain to push the pin in the chain links out. Some chain tools also have a third shorter bolt that can be inserted into the bottom of the downwards channel to prevent a pin from being fully knocked out - insert this if your chain tool has it. Begin tightening the pin bolt down so it starts to push the pin in the links of chain out of the upper outer plate and into the lower channel. If your tool does not have the additional bolt in the lower channel to prevent a pin from being fully pushed out, back out the pin bolt often to check when the short, unwanted section of chain is finally free before the pin is fully pushed out of the bottom outer plate of the link we want. If the pin does pop out of the outermost plate, try again on the next full outer plate. This pin will be pushed back into place around the chain tensioner shortly.





















Remove the bolt previously used to hold the chain in place from the tool and reposition the chain inside the tool with the pin now sitting in the upwards channel. Place an inline chain tensioner inside the outer plates of the chain so that the pin will trap it in place when it is pushed back in. Retighten the locking bolt down so the “cup” at the end of the bolt starts to push the pin back through the outer plate of the chain, and then switch to the longer pin bolt to fully reinsert the pin through both outer plates.


Now the other end of the chain needs to be attached to the other side of the chain tensioner. Make sure the tensioner is attached to one end of the chain and opened about half way. Put the chain over the sprockets such that the tensioner end meets up with the rest of the chain somewhere between the two sprockets to determine the best length of chain (we found it to be around 53”). Find a link with outer plates that makes sense to break the chain at and like before push the pin closer to the unwanted extra chain in the outer link out until the extra chain can be removed and then push the pin back into place around the other end of the chain tensioner. Place the chain loop back over the sprockets, loosening the tensioner for more slack if necessary, and then tighten the tensioner so the chain cannot slip on the sprockets. Repeat this process on the other side.
